Springtime in Ann Arbor

Spring in Ann Arbor is a time of renewal and awakening. The world shakes off the chill of winter, and a vibrant green hue begins to paint the landscape. Though there may still be a nip in the air, the promise of warmer days is enough to draw me outside, eager to explore the beauty that surrounds me. My faithful companion, Bacon, is always ready for an adventure, so we often find ourselves strolling the streets and pathways of our beloved city.


Sometimes our walks take us through the charming neighborhoods of Ann Arbor, where blossoming trees line the streets and cheerful birdsongs fill the air. Other times, we venture deeper into nature, seeking the tranquility of the woods. The dappled sunlight filtering through the leaves, the earthy scent of the forest floor, and the gentle rustle of the wind through the trees create a sense of peace that is both invigorating and calming.


Ann Arbor is blessed with an abundance of natural beauty, and we often find ourselves drawn to the water's edge. The Huron River Watertrail offers stunning views of the river, and the Argo Park Cascades provide a mesmerizing display of rushing water. Whether we're watching ducks glide across a still pond or marveling at the power of a cascading waterfall, these moments spent in nature are a precious gift.


This site is a collection of photographs I've taken during our walks. They capture the essence of springtime in Ann Arbor, from the delicate blossoms of early spring to the lush greenery of late spring. Each image is a reminder of the beauty that surrounds us, and I hope they inspire you to get outside and explore the natural wonders of your own community.
